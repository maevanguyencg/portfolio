The Council: Expected Freedom

Former guard of those rulers, you’re now an investigator working for the rebellion. Description (project)

The Council: Expected Freedom is an investigation and deduction game where you incarnate Evans, a detective working with the rebellion whose goal is to dismantle the politic power in place. As you worked with those politicians before, you have access to many useful resources as an address book, some informers, multiple maps, access to their internal IT system, or badges and cards to lead your inquiries.

The game is divided into 10 main missions that follow each other and reveal the story step by step. And also multiple side quests to earn achievements and additional knowledge.

Each mission starts with a brief to set the context and guide you to start your researches. It is then up to you to choose the best approach to resolve each case as they all can be solved in multiple ways. But be careful, if you take too many risks, you could be trapped, captured, or even killed!

World

Our world was ravaged by a health crisis caused by a virulent and highly contagious virus. As mankind was decimated, the human activity was drastically reduced and everybody focused on finding a vaccine.

After many months of research and coordinate effort, a cure was found and people tried to go back to a normal life. But the economic crisis that followed was too important for it. Every political answer failed to restore peace and hope, and the population started to panic and rebel. In this chaotic atmosphere, a young and ambitious man named Alan took the power by force and convinced the powerful individuals of the world to support him.

In only a few weeks, he became the new leader of the entire world, and in several months he imposed his tyranny.

Now, humanity is divided into two, the riches and powerful in one side, and the poor and weak in the other. The firsts get everything by enslaving the seconds who work hard and barely survive.

The rebellion grows in the population, and it is now time to restore equality and to turn the tables!
